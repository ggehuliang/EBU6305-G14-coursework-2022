    Unit 1 Cradle of Civilization

    Shaanxi, located in the middle reaches of the Yellow River, is the birthplace of the Chinese nation and one of the cradles of ancient civilization. The ancient human remains found in Shaanxi reflect the formation, evolution, survival and development of the ancient ancestors here, and reproduce the basic features of Shaanxi's prehistoric civilization and the vibrant childhood of the Chinese nation.

    Unit 2 Hepzong Zhou

    The Western Zhou was an important stage in the development of early Chinese statehood. Its political system, economic form, and especially its ethical spirit had a profound influence on future generations Shaanxi was the royal capital and political center of the Western Zhou, and the rich relics and monuments reproduce the brilliant Western Zhou civilization.

    Unit 3 Eastern Empire

    The Qin people are a group of Chinese people who used to be active in the present-day Tianshui area of Gansu. The cultural relics of the Qin Dynasty, represented by the Terracotta Warriors and Horses, show the strong pioneering consciousness and enterprising spirit of the Qin people with their majestic grandeur and distinctive military features.

    Unit 4 The Great Han Dynasty

    The Western Han Dynasty, which settled in Chang'an, created the first prosperous era of ancient Chinese society. The Han capital of Chang'an, the Han tombs and a large number of Han relics reproduce the developed and prosperous society and economy, the colorful social life, the majestic culture and art, and the vigorous spirit of the times.
