The tiger tally was designed to be split in half. The Emperor held the right half, whilst governor or general in Du county held the other half. It was used exclusively to mobilize armies in ancient China. The left half of the tally is in the shape of a walking tiger. When fitted together with the right half, a troop unit of more than 50 soldiers could be dispatched. It was the symbol of highly centralized military leadership in Qin Dynasty.

This vessel for ladling out wine was crafted in a unique style. On its handle there is a tiger preparing to catch a sheep. The mould as a whole presents a very vivid style.

With a handle in the shape of a horse head, this sword is a gem of a collection of articles for daily use from a minority nationality of the Shang Dynasty, called Gui Fang. This kind of artifact has also been excavated in Qinglong of Hebei Province, Shilou of Shanxi Province,the Fuhao’s tomb of Yinxu Ruins at Anyang, Henan Province. Using the head of a snake, horsesheep as the handle of a knife or sword is the decorative feature of this nomadic tirbe from Northern China.
